# References and Bibliography

This template provides the structure for the textbook's references and bibliography.

## Books

[1] Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of work*. Publisher.

[2] Author, B. B. (Year). *Title of work: Subtitle*. Publisher.

## Journal Articles

[3] Author, C. C. (Year). Title of article. *Title of Periodical*, Volume(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/xx.xxxx/xxxxx

[4] Author, D. D., & Author, E. E. (Year). Title of article. *Title of Periodical*, Volume(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/xx.xxxx/xxxxx

## Conference Papers

[5] Author, F. F. (Year). Title of paper. In *Proceedings of the Conference Name* (pp. pages). Publisher. https://doi.org/xx.xxxx/xxxxx

## Technical Reports

[6] Author, G. G. (Year). Title of report (Report No. XXXX). Institution.

## Websites

[7] Name of Website. (Year). Title of specific document. URL

## Preprints/ArXiv

[8] Author, H. H., & Author, I. I. (Year). Title of preprint. *arXiv preprint*. arXiv:xxxx.xxxxx

## Standards

[9] Organization. (Year). *Standard title* (Standard number). Publisher.

---

## Notes

- All references should follow a consistent citation style (e.g., APA, IEEE)
- Ensure all in-text citations match entries in this bibliography
- Regularly update references with the latest research
- Consider including links to open-access versions when available
